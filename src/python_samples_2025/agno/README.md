## What is it agno?

Agno is a lightweight library for building Multimodal Agents. It exposes LLMs as a unified API and gives them superpowers like memory, knowledge, tools and reasoning.

https://docs.agno.com/introduction

https://github.com/agno-agi/agno

┃                                                                                                                                                                                  ┃
┃ Here's a recipe for Chicken and Galangal in Coconut Milk Soup (Tom Kha Gai):                                                                                                     ┃
┃                                                                                                                                                                                  ┃
┃                                                                            Ingredients (One serving)                                                                             ┃
┃                                                                                                                                                                                  ┃
┃  • 150 grams chicken, cut into bite-size pieces                                                                                                                                  ┃
┃  • 50 grams sliced young galangal                                                                                                                                                ┃
┃  • 100 grams lightly crushed lemongrass, julienned                                                                                                                               ┃
┃  • 100 grams straw mushrooms                                                                                                                                                     ┃
┃  • 250 grams coconut milk                                                                                                                                                        ┃
┃  • 100 grams chicken stock                                                                                                                                                       ┃
┃  • 3 tbsp lime juice                                                                                                                                                             ┃
┃  • 3 tbsp fish sauce                                                                                                                                                             ┃
┃  • 2 leaves kaffir lime, shredded                                                                                                                                                ┃
┃  • 1-2 bird’s eye chilies, pounded                                                                                                                                               ┃
┃  • 3 leaves coriander                                                                                                                                                            ┃
┃                                                                                                                                                                                  ┃
┃                                                                                    Directions                                                                                    ┃
┃                                                                                                                                                                                  ┃
┃  1 Preparation: Gather and prepare all ingredients.                                                                                                                              ┃
┃  2 Boil: Bring the chicken stock and coconut milk to a slow boil. Add galangal, lemongrass, chicken, and mushrooms.                                                              ┃
┃  3 Season: When the soup returns to a boil, season it with fish sauce.                                                                                                           ┃
┃  4 Cook Chicken: Wait until the chicken is cooked, then add the kaffir lime leaves and bird's eye chilies.                                                                       ┃
┃  5 Add Lime Juice: Remove the pot from heat and add lime juice.                                                                                                                  ┃
┃  6 Garnish: Garnish with coriander leaves.                                                                                                                                       ┃
┃                                                                                                                                                                                  ┃
┃                                                                                       Tips                                                                                       ┃
┃                                                                                                                                                                                  ┃
┃  • Keep the heat low throughout the cooking process to prevent the oil in the coconut milk from separating.                                                                      ┃
┃  • If you’re using mature galangal, reduce the amount.                                                                                                                           ┃
┃  • Lime juice becomes more aromatic when added after removing the pot from heat.                                                                                                 ┃
┃  • Reduce the amount of chilies for a milder taste.                                                                                                                              ┃
┃                                                                                                                                                                                  ┃
┃ Enjoy your Thai culinary experience!                                                                                                                                             ┃

┃                                                                              Origins and Influences                                                                              ┃
┃                                                                                                                                                                                  ┃
┃  • Historical Influence: The roots of Thai curry can be traced back to the influence of Indian and Malaysian cuisines. Indian traders and Buddhist missionaries are believed to  ┃
┃    have introduced the use of curry spices to Thailand. The trade routes were conduits not just for commerce, but also for cultural exchanges. Over time, these Indian curry     ┃
┃    influences were adapted to Thai palates and local ingredients.                                                                                                                ┃
┃  • Key Ingredients: Unlike Indian curries that typically use dry spices, Thai curries are characterized by the use of fresh ingredients such as lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves,  ┃
┃    galangal, and herbs. Coconut milk, a prevalent ingredient in Thai curries, was introduced as an alternative to the ghee used in Indian curries, creating a unique texture and ┃
┃    richness.                                                                                                                                                                     ┃
┃                                                                                                                                                                                  ┃
┃                                                                              Types of Thai Curries                                                                               ┃
┃                                                                                                                                                                                  ┃
┃ Thai curries are broadly categorized by their color and base ingredients:                                                                                                        ┃
┃                                                                                                                                                                                  ┃
┃  • Green Curry (Gaeng Kiew Wan): This is known for its aromatic, spicy, and sweet notes. The green color comes from green chilies, and it includes a blend of fresh herbs.       ┃
┃  • Red Curry: Made from red chilies, it is typically spicier than green curry. It includes ingredients like tomatoes or red chilies to give it a bold color.                     ┃
┃  • Massaman Curry: With roots that blend Middle Eastern influences evident in the use of spices like cardamom, cinnamon, and star anise, Massaman curry is often made with beef  ┃
┃    or chicken.                                                                                                                                                                   ┃
┃  • Yellow Curry: Similar to some Indian curries but with Thai ingredients like kaffir lime and lemongrass, it's less spicy and more aromatic.                                    ┃
┃                                                                                                                                                                                  ┃
┃                                                                                    Evolution                                                                                     ┃
┃                                                                                                                                                                                  ┃
┃ In the past, curries in Thailand were simpler, often merely boiled dishes with a broth base. As time passed and regional variations developed, Thai culinary practices advanced. ┃
┃ This evolution continued with the introduction of new cooking techniques and fusion with other regional practices.                                                               ┃
┃                                                                                                                                                                                  ┃
┃                                                                              Cultural Significance                                                                               ┃
┃                                                                                                                                                                                  ┃
┃ Thai curries hold cultural significance, often served during family gatherings and religious ceremonies. They represent the delicate balance of flavors that defines Thai        ┃
┃ cuisine: a combination of spicy, sweet, sour, and salty.                                                                                                                         ┃
